Introduction

General Guide

In the 1950s and 1970s, there were about 2,000 troops stationed in Jiangmei Village. It used to be a prosperous and lively town with flourishing agricultural, commercial and fishing industries. At the same time, most of the residents were self-sufficient enough to grow fruits and vegetables, raise pigs or other livestock, fish, or do business. After the military base moved out in 1970, a large number of soldiers moved out, and the population declined sharply. The town’s current population is around 700 people, with around 300 residents, mainly Wu.
